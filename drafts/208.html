An aquagraphics-style water curtain.

208. An aquagraphic-style water curtain that displays your team name or logo. [250 points] †

This is one of the items I worked on for Scav this year. I didn't complete it. At the time I felt terrible about letting down the team. I promised myself I would complete it, someday.

Today is not that day.

Today I write about the original design. What went wrong (easy to do), what went right (also important), and most importantly, why I want to build version 2.

First of all, thanks to all the people (Andrew, Catrina, Cecilie, and Ryan) who helped this thing get even partway complete.

My original concept was a small wooden frame, about 18" tall and 12" wide. Across the top, I mounted a piece of PVC as the water reservoir. 1/8" holes were drilled at 1/2" intervals in the bottom of the pipe to let the water out. 20 solenoids were mounted side-by-side next to the pipe; these slid 1/4" in and out when I pulsed electricity through them. Pieces of foamcore were attached to these solenoids to act as crude valves for the water. As for control, all the solenoids had a common ground. Their positive leads scrubbed against a rotating drum. This lets you use the drum as a sort of music-box: if you put patches of conductive material (aluminum foil, conductive paint, etc.) on it, they will act as switches. Whenever the leads touch the conductive material, the circuit will be closed and the solenoid will slide out, opening the valve and letting water through. If you draw a conductive picture on the drum, as it rotates the valves will open and close with the proper timings to translate that picture to water.

This sounds great (I hope)! I was really excited about it - not only would we be able to display our own team logo, we would be able to draw anything we wanted and turn it into water!
