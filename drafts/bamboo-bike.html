Bamboo Bike

In high school I built 2 bamboo bikes. The second one broke and I rebuilt it.

Here are their stories.

v0.1

One day I was riding home from school with my friend Chris. I said to him that we should build a bamboo bike in the summer. He said that that was a great idea and that we should do it.

When summer started we roamed around Palo Alto, schmoozing for bike parts.

Somebody gave us an old frame that had been in a crash.

We sawed the joints off of it.

Then we went to a bamboo garden in the hills and got some bamboo.

We heat-treated the bamboo with a heat gun. This both dries the bamboo and strengthens it. Then we cut it into appropriate lengths and fit them onto the steel joints.

The bamboo was loose on the steel tubes, so we filled the space with expanding foam. Then we mixed up some epoxy resin, soaked some twine in it, and wrapped that around the joints to strengthen them.

The final product had some pretty wonky-looking geometry, which was totally different from what we had laid out earlier:

The bottom bracket holder was very low to the ground, resulting in low ground clearance. I had to be very careful to avoid pedal strike. I resolved to use a jig the next time around.

v0.2

The next summer, I decided to build another bike, taking the lessons from last time into account. I was pretty pleased with the result:

I made an Instructable about the build process.

I rode this daily for several months, then one day the bottom bracket joint snapped as I was crossing a street. So I started thinking about the next one.

v0.3

For this one I redid the joints that broke with carbon fiber:

First I tacked the joints back together. Then I built up the joints with long-fiber Bondo, an epoxy paste with fiberglass. I smoothed the Bondo out with a Dremel, and started laminating the joint with carbon fiber. I had procured a vacuum pump, made an air filter with activated charcoal and a metal can, and bought some trash-bags. I was able to do some crude vacuum-bagging of the joints. As I was waiting for the epoxy to cure, I thought of a couple other ways I could have joined the bamboo that I'd like to test the strength of someday.
